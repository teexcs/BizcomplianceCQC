**Would I buy on a first visit?** No — but I would *want* to. I'd run the free checker, take the score, and then email you instead of paying, because I can't verify who I'm paying. That's the whole story of this site: **the product persuades, the entity doesn't.**

**Does it look worth £595?** The *offer* does. The *seller* doesn't yet. Price perception is fine — £595 against the cost of a "Requires Improvement" rating is obviously cheap. What's missing is the safety to hand it to a stranger.

## The three silent questions (and your current answers)

A care buyer spending the service's money asks three things without ever saying them:

1. **"Do they actually know CQC?"** → **Yes, emphatically.** The specificity is your moat: "lone working, Schedule 3 recruitment files, travel-buffered rotas," "best-interests decisions," "Reg 20A rating display." No generic compliance shop writes like this.
2. **"Has this worked for anyone real?"** → **No evidence.** Case studies are labelled *"anonymised composites"* — the buyer correctly reads: fiction. Zero testimonials, zero named clients, zero review platform, zero "X services audited."
3. **"Who am I paying, and can I chase them if it goes wrong?"** → **Unanswerable.** No company number, no registered address, no phone, no named human anywhere. The founder is *"a UK law graduate (LLB, London)"* — anonymous. The About page claims a team of specialists with no names.

One yes out of three ≈ 67/100. That's the maths of this score.

## What genuinely works — protect these

- **The clarity discipline.** Every page repeats the same offer in the same terms. This is rarer and more valuable than it looks.
- **The free website checker.** A working tool on a consultant-style site is a category-breaking differentiator. Nobody at your size in UK care compliance has this. It's also your lead engine — every check is an email or a warm visitor.
- **The funnel architecture.** Free check → £8.99 report → £595 audit → monthly retention (gated behind the audit) is a textbook value ladder. The "monthly plans unlock after the audit" gate is genuinely clever: it makes the audit the only door, which is exactly your strategy.
- **Honesty as positioning.** "We are not the CQC. We never promise a pass." Keep every word. In this sector, over-promising is what scammers do; caution reads as competence.
- **The dashboard preview ("Scoring") section.** Showing the score dial, RAG chips and action plan rows makes the deliverable *feel* tangible pre-purchase.
- **Sector segmentation.** The five sector packs with genuinely different pain points show range without diluting the domiciliary-first depth.

## What's blocking the sale — strict and prioritised

### 🔴 P0 — before you spend anything on traffic

**1. You fail your own scanner.** Your product's headline feature checks websites for: company registration number, physical address, contact details. Your site has **none of the three** (footer: "BizCompliance CQC Ltd" with no number; contact page: email + form only; no postcode anywhere). Any buyer who runs your checker on your own domain — and some will, it's the obvious test — watches you fail your own audit. This converts your best asset into your worst liability. *Fix: company number + registered address + phone in the footer, today.*

**2. Zero third-party proof.** Not one real quote. The fix does not require scale: run **three pilot audits free or at cost** in exchange for a named, role-attributed testimonial ("— Registered Manager, domiciliary service, Kent") and one permissioned, redacted report page. Three quotes and one real artefact move this site more than any redesign could.

**3. The About page actively damages you.** It's written for a different business — *"SMBs," "every UK business," "data protection advisors"* — and its anonymous-founder-plus-unnamed-team framing reads as *no team*. After eight pages of tight CQC copy, this is where a diligent buyer goes to check who you are, and it's the weakest page on the site. Rewrite it CQC-native, with your name, your face, and the honest credential line you already use elsewhere.

### 🟠 P1 — costing conversions now

**4. The 48-hour promise dissolves under inspection.** Homepage: "In 48 hours." FAQ: *"the review is completed after we receive your evidence"* — no clock. Careful buyers notice. Define it once, use it everywhere: **"48 hours from the moment your evidence upload is complete."**

**5. "Backed by a specialist care compliance library"** is the one place you got vague where you used to be concrete. The number (139-document library) was doing real work. Put a number back.

**6. No phone, no bookable call.** This is a considered B2B purchase in a phone-first sector. A "book a 10-minute call" link (even just Calendly) would catch every buyer currently bouncing at the trust gap.

**7. No risk reversal on the £595.** You don't need a refund promise if you don't want one — even framing works: *"A single missed legal item found early pays for the audit many times over. A downgraded rating costs far more."* One sentence near the price.

### 🟡 P2 — polish

**8. "Sign-Up Is Live Now."** in the hero card is filler that reads slightly amateur — of course sign-up is live. Use that line for something that sells ("No card required" / "Evidence vault opens immediately").
**9. The stats strip's "100% Evidence review"** is weaker than the old concrete number — "100%" of what? Consider "139 evidence points" again, or "18/18 areas covered."
**10. Everything visual is hypothetical.** The 76/100 dashboard, action plan rows, case scores — all mock. Fine and normal pre-launch, but it compounds the proof problem until finding #2 is fixed.

## The five moves that change the number

1. **Pass your own scanner** — company number, address, phone in the footer. (30 minutes.)
2. **Three pilot audits → three named testimonials + one redacted real report page.** (2 weeks.)
3. **Rewrite About**: CQC-native, named founder, photo, honest credential. (1 evening.)
4. **Pin the 48h clock** to "from completed evidence upload," everywhere including FAQ. (30 minutes.)
5. **Add a phone number / bookable call + one risk-reversal sentence at the price.** (1 hour.)

Do these and the same site scores **82–85/100** — because the deficit was never the product, the copy, or the design. It's that a stranger cannot yet verify you. Everything else is already better than most funded competitors' sites.
