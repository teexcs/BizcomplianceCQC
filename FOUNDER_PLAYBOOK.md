**The uncomfortable truth from the sector:** nobody big got there selling one-off audits. Audits churn 100% by definition. QCS's entire empire is the *subscription to not worry*. Your one-off audit is the correct wedge **only if** you treat audit→monthly attach rate as the single number the business lives or dies on. Design everything — the report, the delivery call, the follow-up emails — to make the monthly plan the obvious next breath.

## 2. The 90-day plan (exact steps, in order)

### Days 1–7 — Make yourself verifiable (free, and it's blocking everything)
1. Footer: company number, registered office, phone. **Run your own scanner on your own site until it scores 9+.** Screenshot the result — that screenshot is a marketing asset ("we score 9.8 on our own audit — what do you score?").
2. Rewrite About: CQC-native, your name, your face, "law graduate — not a solicitor, and we say so" framing. One page, one evening.
3. Pin the 48h promise: "48 hours from completed evidence upload" — homepage, pricing, FAQ, report.
4. Apply migrations 0001–0005 + seed the library + set the two Stripe products (£595, £8.99) live per SETUP.md. **The machine must be able to take money before you promote anything.**
5. Set up Trustpilot (free tier) and a Calendly "10-minute readiness call" link on Contact + Pricing.

### Days 8–21 — Manufacture proof
6. **Three pilot audits, free, hand-picked** — one domiciliary, one supported living, one new provider. Price of admission: a named/role-attributed testimonial, permission for one redacted report page, and a Trustpilot review. Deliver them exceptionally; these three write your next six months of marketing.
7. Publish the three quotes + one redacted report page on the site. Case studies page: swap one composite for one real (permissioned) story.
8. While delivering the pilots, **document your own delivery workflow** in the admin engine — every friction you hit now is what breaks your 48h SLA at 5 audits/week later.

### Days 22–45 — Turn on demand (two channels only)
9. **Channel A — the CQC new-registration list.** CQC publishes newly registered providers and registered-manager changes as public data, updated monthly. New providers are your perfect buyer: pre-first-inspection, anxious, no incumbent. Build a simple weekly routine: pull new domiciliary/supported-living registrations → run your scanner on their website → send a short, personal email: *"We ran a compliance check on your website — you scored 4.2/10 and you're missing your Reg 20A rating display, which carries a fixed penalty. Here's the free summary. When you're ready to check the other 139 evidence points, that's what we do."* This is the highest-leverage move available to you: fresh list monthly, perfect intent, and your tool does the door-opening. (I can build this pipeline for you — see §4.)
10. **Channel B — founder-led LinkedIn + RM communities.** 3 posts/week, all practitioner-grade specificity (the same voice as your site): "The 5 Schedule 3 gaps that fail recruitment files," "Reg 20A: the legal duty most care websites break." Registered-manager Facebook groups and care forums allow tools when they're genuinely free — share the scanner, never the sales page.
11. Wire the follow-up: scanner leads already land in your database with email + score. Send day-3 and day-10 follow-ups (score reminder → audit pitch). You have the data; it just needs the sequence.

### Days 46–90 — Build the moat and the ladder
12. **SEO hub, 10 pages, written once, compounding forever:** the CQC mock inspection checklist, all-68-SAF-questions explained, Reg 20A guide, Schedule 3 recruitment file checklist, "new provider first inspection" guide, one page per sector pack. Every page ends in the free scanner. This is the QCS playbook at 1% of their cost.
13. **Raise the audit price** to £695–£795 once you have 5+ testimonials — your review found price perception is not the objection; trust was. Grandfather nothing; the scanner + proof justify it.
14. **Attach-rate machinery:** the delivered report's last page and the delivery email must both present the monthly plan as "keep this score maintained" — and offer the first month at a founding rate. Target: ≥30% of audit buyers on a plan within 30 days. Below 15%? The monthly offer is wrong, fix it before scaling traffic.
15. **One partnership experiment:** local care association or a care-home consultant who doesn't serve domiciliary — white-label conversations become your Partner tier launch case when you're ready to unlock it.

### The only numbers that matter (check weekly)
- Scanner runs → email capture rate (target 25%+)
- Email → audit purchase (target 3–5% within 30 days)
- **Audit → monthly attach (target 30%; the business-defining metric)**
- Monthly churn (target <3%/mo; care providers rarely leave things that remove anxiety)
- Audits delivered inside 48h SLA (must be 100% — it's the brand)

## 3. Decisions I'd make that you haven't asked about

- **Don't unlock Partner yet** (you already decided this — it's right). Multi-workspace before single-workspace retention is proven is how solo founders drown.
- **Don't add sectors.** Domiciliary + supported living until £5k MRR. Every extra sector halves your specificity, which is your only current moat.
- **Do the audits yourself for the first 25.** The engine makes each one faster, and each one teaches the engine (your matcher, crosswalk and findings templates improve with every real audit). Hire a part-time reviewer only when you're refusing work.
- **Never compete with QCS on library size.** They have 2,500+ documents and 20 years. You win on *the audit verdict + the score + the 48 hours* — an outcome they don't sell. Position against them explicitly: "QCS gives you policies. We tell you if you'd pass."
- **Keep the £8.99 report exactly as it is** — its job is not revenue, it's (a) qualifying buyers who'll pay for compliance and (b) making the £595 feel like the obvious serious version. Watch the £8.99→£595 upgrade rate.
